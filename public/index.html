Overview
Poverty, perhaps a relatively unfamiliar term for some, is still troubling a substantial number of people in the world.
In 2019, stil 1,800,000,000 people lived below the $3.65 per day poverty line.
More than 648,000,000 people did not even have $2.15 per day for living.

In terms of poverty, there are various topics for us to research on.
In this work, I focus on the inequity of the world with regard to poverty.

Until now, there is no unified standard for poverty, for each country's living standard and costs for essential supplements are different.
For instance, the housing price in Shanghai is higher than in Stuttgart, while cabbage in Stuttgart could be 5-time more expensive.
Nevertheless, some thresholds can still be used to measure poverty, such as the UN poverty line.
And if you look at some of the charts below, you will find that the inequity gap is too large to be explained as differences in standards.
